# Quizzes

**Stage:** Communication | **Mode:** Conversational | **Targets:** Unknown unknowns

## What it does

Tests your understanding of a topic through adaptive Q&A to expose the dangerous kind of
unknown — things you think you understand but actually don't.

## When to use it

- When learning something new and want to verify retention
- Before a presentation or discussion to find weak spots
- After reading an article or document to test comprehension
- When you suspect you have surface understanding but not depth

## How it works

1. You specify the topic to be tested on
2. The agent generates questions across cognitive levels (Bloom's taxonomy)
3. You answer one at a time, getting immediate feedback
4. Difficulty adapts — correct answers raise it, incorrect lower it
5. After 8-12 questions, you receive a gap map

## Key mechanism

**Adaptive targeting.** The quiz zeros in on the boundary between "knows" and "doesn't know."
It doesn't waste time on things you clearly understand or things clearly beyond your level. It
finds the edge — that's where the blind spots live.

**Cognitive levels, not just facts.** Questions test six levels:
- Recall (can you remember it?)
- Comprehension (can you explain it?)
- Application (can you use it in a new situation?)
- Analysis (can you break it down?)
- Synthesis (can you combine it with other ideas?)
- Evaluation (can you judge and defend?)

You might score well on recall but poorly on application — that gap tells you exactly what kind
of practice you need.

**Bloom's progression reveals false confidence.** Many people can recall facts but can't apply
them. The quiz exposes exactly where comprehension breaks down.

## Output

Score + cognitive level performance map + specific knowledge gaps identified + recommended next
steps for each gap.

## Connects to

- After this → `interviews` to explore weak areas from expert perspectives
- After this → `references` to find study material for knowledge gaps
- Before this ← `pitches` (verify understanding of areas where messaging was inconsistent)
