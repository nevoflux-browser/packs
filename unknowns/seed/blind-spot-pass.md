# Blind Spot Pass

**Stage:** Discovery | **Mode:** Probing | **Targets:** Unknown unknowns

## What it does

Scans your plan or idea from multiple dynamically-chosen perspectives to find gaps, assumptions,
and risks you haven't examined.

## When to use it

- Before committing to a plan or decision
- When you feel confident but want a sanity check
- When stakes are high and you can't afford to miss something
- After brainstorming, before building a plan

## How it works

1. You provide a plan, idea, or proposal
2. The agent proposes 5-7 scanning perspectives tailored to your topic
3. You confirm or adjust the perspectives — seeing the list is itself a discovery moment
4. The agent scans from each perspective, identifying blind spots
5. You receive a severity-sorted checklist of gaps and assumptions

## Key mechanism

The perspectives are not a fixed checklist. They are dynamically generated to match your topic.
A product idea gets different perspectives than a career decision or a research approach.

The act of seeing the proposed perspectives is itself valuable — if a perspective surprises you,
that's already a blind spot revealed.

## Output

A structured blind spot report: critical blind spots (address now), notable gaps (investigate),
and assumptions to validate (test).

## Connects to

- After this → `brainstorming` to explore solutions for the gaps found
- After this → `interviews` to get deeper perspectives on specific blind spots
- Before this ← `brainstorming` (scan a chosen direction for blind spots before committing)
