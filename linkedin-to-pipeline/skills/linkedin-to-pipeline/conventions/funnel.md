# Funnel — the 70/20/10 mix

The whole system rests on one idea: **content is the funnel**, not the top of it. Buyers
research your profile and your posts before they ever take a call, so the mix of what you
publish is what moves someone from stranger to conversation.

Map every post to a funnel stage and run roughly a **70 / 20 / 10** split across a rolling
window (see `calendar-arc.md` for how the window and spacing work). The ratio below is the
default; a user may override it in `linkedin-to-pipeline/brand-profile`.

## TOFU — 70% — goal: reach

The top of the funnel earns the attention the rest of the system spends. Formats:

- Personal stories and milestones
- Strong opinions, and the occasional contrarian take
- Resource lists and behind-the-scenes moments

## MOFU — 20% — goal: show how you think

The middle turns reach into trust by demonstrating judgment. Formats:

- Frameworks and process breakdowns
- Cheat sheets, carousels, and tech-stack posts
- How-tos and step-by-step workflows

## BOFU — 10% — goal: start a conversation

The bottom converts trust into meetings. Formats:

- Case studies and client results
- Product demos and feature walkthroughs
- Free-audit offers and direct handraiser posts

## Why the ratio matters

The three stages depend on each other in order:

> TOFU earns the reach that MOFU converts into trust, and BOFU is where that trust finally
> turns into meetings.

Get the ratio wrong and one of two failure modes shows up:

- **All TOFU** → big reach, lots of likes, and zero pipeline.
- **All BOFU** → every post reads like an ad to an audience that doesn't trust you yet.

The 70/20/10 split is what keeps both failure modes off the table. BOFU only works once TOFU
and MOFU have done their job — which is why sequencing (in `calendar-arc.md`) never lets a
BOFU post run cold.
