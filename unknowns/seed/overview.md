# Unknowns Toolkit

A thinking toolkit for discovering what you don't know you don't know.

## The core insight

Every project, decision, or learning endeavor has three types of unknowns:

| Type | Description | Danger level |
|------|------------|-------------|
| **Known unknowns** | Things you know you need to figure out | Low — you're already tracking them |
| **Unknown knowns** | Things you know but can't articulate | Medium — latent knowledge goes unused |
| **Unknown unknowns** | Things you don't know you don't know | High — invisible until it's too late |

Different techniques target different types. Using the wrong technique for the wrong type wastes time.

## The 4-stage model

### Discovery — What haven't I considered?
- **Blind Spot Pass** (probing) — Multi-perspective scan for gaps
- **Brainstorming** (conversational) — Generate options to activate latent preferences

### Research — What exists that I don't know about?
- **Interviews** (conversational) — Role-play perspectives to find blind spots
- **References** (probing) — Find and analyze existing examples

### Execution — How do I organize and track what I know?
- **Plans** (structured generation) — Turn ideas into prioritized, certainty-annotated plans
- **Notes** (structured generation) — Record decisions and deviations during execution

### Communication — Can I explain and defend what I've done?
- **Pitches** (structured generation) — Package work for specific audiences
- **Quizzes** (conversational) — Test understanding to find knowledge gaps

## When to use what

| Situation | Start with |
|-----------|-----------|
| "I have an idea but I'm not sure it's good" | blind-spot-pass |
| "I don't know what I want" | brainstorming |
| "I need other perspectives" | interviews |
| "I've seen something like what I want" | references |
| "I need to organize my approach" | plans |
| "I'm in the middle of executing" | notes |
| "I need to explain this to someone" | pitches |
| "Do I actually understand this?" | quizzes |

## How techniques connect

Techniques are not isolated. One's output naturally feeds another:

- blind-spot-pass finds gaps → brainstorming explores solutions
- brainstorming converges → plans structures the approach
- interviews surface concerns → blind-spot-pass digs deeper
- plans identifies low-certainty steps → interviews or references fill gaps
- notes documents execution → pitches packages for stakeholders
- pitches reveals framing gaps → quizzes verifies understanding
