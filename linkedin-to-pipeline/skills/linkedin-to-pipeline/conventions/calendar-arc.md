# Calendar & arc — rolling window, one-time ramp, spacing rules

Two time concepts are easy to confuse. Keep them separate.

> **Note on defaults:** the 90-day arc length and the 2–3-posts-per-week cadence below are
> sensible defaults, **not rules from the source methodology**. The methodology fixes the
> *ratio* (70/20/10) and rewards *repeated performance*; the calendar shape is ours to tune.
> Both live as knobs in `linkedin-to-pipeline/brand-profile`.

## 1. The ratio is a rolling-window property

70/20/10 (see `funnel.md`) is maintained over a **rolling window of roughly the last 10
posts** — not per fixed calendar block. In steady state, any recent window of posts holds the
mix. You never "reset" the ratio at a date boundary.

## 2. The arc is a one-time ramp, not a cycle

The arc **positioning → authority → conversion** is a **one-time maturity ramp**, run on a
cold start or when repositioning:

- **Positioning** — establish who you help and your angle (TOFU-heavy).
- **Authority** — prove how you think (more MOFU).
- **Conversion** — invite the conversation (BOFU enters).

Once you've ramped, you're in steady state — the arc does not repeat every 90 days.
`linkedin-calendar-planning` runs the arc once, then only re-plans/tops-up open slots.

## 3. Ordering within a window — interleave by ratio, with spacing rules

Posts are **not** rotated in a rigid T/M/B sequence (the mix is 7:2:1, not 1:1:1), and **not**
random (random clusters BOFU or fires it cold). Instead:

- **TOFU is the backbone** — most posts each week.
- **MOFU ≈ every 5th post.**
- **BOFU ≈ every 10th post** — spaced out, and **always following a run of trust-building
  content**, never cold. A BOFU ask only lands after TOFU/MOFU have earned the trust.
- Each post's exact stage and format are chosen by **what the idea needs**, inside the ratio
  budget for the window.

## 4. Backlog strategy

Keep the `idea-backlog` deeper than the open slots so `post-write` is never starved — but mine
**toward the open slots** (`idea-mining` is slot-targeted), so the backlog stays balanced to
the ratio rather than filling up with whatever was easiest to think of.
